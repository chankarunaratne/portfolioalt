Form follows function

Design should always be meaningful. A good-looking interface should never come at the expense of the problem it's meant to solve.

Steve Jobs famously said, "Design is not just what it looks like and feels like. Design is how it works."

Less is more

The beauty in usability lies in its simplicity. A product becomes easier to understand, faster to interact with, and delightful to use when you get rid of its complexity.

Giving users more power doesn't always mean adding new things; it can also mean removing unnecessary noise.

Craft is in the detail

Small but thoughtful additions to a design add up to making a beautifully crafted product. Carefully designed microcopy, consistent behaviour, and animations all shape a product that feels trustworthy.

Give attention to your craft, and users will respond to it without even realising the details.

Never stop learning

Design is an ever evolving craft. The tools we use, needs of the user, or the technology of our time is always changing.

It's vital that we wake up every day filled with curiosity and a drive to learn and grow.

Own your work

I believe every team member should be an equal owner of the product. Be proud to have the burden of reponsibility. You get to be a part of building something special.
